# Worker FAQ — FLOSTRUCTION

**Last updated:** [populate on first publish]
**Audience:** Construction workers using FLOSTRUCTION for the first time.

### What is FLOSTRUCTION?

FLOSTRUCTION is a verified-hours record for construction workers. You tap to start your shift, tap to take a break, and tap to end your shift. Your supervisor confirms your hours by SMS. The result is a permanent record — timestamped to the second, GPS-confirmed to your work site, and sealed against changes by anyone.

FLOSTRUCTION does not calculate your pay, your award rate, or your superannuation. It records your hours accurately and keeps them safe. Your employer uses those hours to calculate your pay. If those calculations are ever wrong, your sealed FLOSTRUCTION record is the evidence that proves what you actually worked.

You keep a copy of your records forever — even after you leave your employer. You can download them as a spreadsheet or as PDF receipts at any time.

---

### Why do I get a text message?

When you finish a shift and tap CLOCK_OUT, your supervisor gets an SMS asking them to confirm your hours. FLOSTRUCTION is built around SMS so your supervisor does not need to download an app or create an account. They reply YES [shift code] to approve, or QUERY [shift code] if something looks wrong.

The text message is not what creates your record. Your shift is recorded the moment you tap CLOCK_OUT — that record is already sealed before your supervisor responds. The SMS is how your supervisor formally approves the shift. Approval is required before the shift is counted as verified, but the underlying record cannot be changed whether your supervisor approves it, queries it, or does not respond at all.

If your supervisor does not respond within 24 hours, email FLOSMOSIS at support@flosmosis.com and we will follow up on your behalf.

### What is a "sealed shift"?

A sealed shift is a shift record that cannot be changed. The moment you tap CLOCK_OUT, your times and GPS location are locked with a unique fingerprint — a long string of letters and numbers calculated from the exact contents of the record. If anyone changes a single piece of that information, the fingerprint stops matching and the tampering is immediately visible to anyone who checks.

Nobody can quietly alter a sealed shift — not your supervisor, not your employer, not FLOSMOSIS. If anyone tried, the verification check would fail visibly. There is no back door.

This matters because it means your record is yours, permanently, exactly as you tapped it. In any dispute about your hours, your sealed FLOSTRUCTION record is the version that holds up — not someone's recollection, not a revised spreadsheet.

For a plain-English explanation of how the fingerprint works, read [What "sealed" means](/field/seal).

### My pay slip says different hours — what do I do?

Open FLOSTRUCTION, tap "My records," and find the shift in question. Your sealed record shows the exact time you tapped CLOCK_IN and CLOCK_OUT, along with GPS confirmation that you were on site at those times.

If the hours on your pay slip do not match your sealed record, screenshot both — your FLOSTRUCTION shift detail and your pay slip — and keep copies somewhere you can find them. Start with your employer or labour-hire company. Most discrepancies are payroll processing errors that get fixed once you flag them in writing.

If your employer does not fix it, or if the difference is significant and you believe you are being underpaid, the Fair Work Ombudsman at fairwork.gov.au is free to use. They have investigators who can require your employer to produce records. Your sealed FLOSTRUCTION record is independent evidence — your employer does not hold it and cannot alter it.

For award rate questions or entitlement disputes that go beyond hours, the Fair Work Infoline is 13 13 94 and calls are free.

### I disagree with how my supervisor approved my shift

If your supervisor has approved a shift but the approved version does not match what you actually worked — or if they have flagged a shift you believe is correct — you can raise a dispute directly with FLOSMOSIS. The dispute does not go through your supervisor.

Open the affected shift in the app and tap "Dispute this shift." Add a brief description of what you believe went wrong. FLOSMOSIS reviews the sealed record and the GPS evidence and responds to you within one business day.

Your supervisor cannot change the sealed record. All they can do is approve or query it. If you have a dispute, FLOSMOSIS is the independent reviewer — we look at the same evidence your supervisor saw and make our own assessment.

For a full explanation of your dispute rights, including what happens after a FLOSMOSIS review, see [Your rights](/field/rights).

### Can my shift be changed after it is sealed?

No. The underlying record cannot be changed by anyone — not your supervisor, not your employer, not FLOSMOSIS.

Your supervisor can approve or query your shift, and you can attach a flag to a shift to note your disagreement. But neither of those actions changes the sealed record. The times you tapped and the GPS points at those times are permanent. Any approval or flag sits on top of the sealed record; the record itself does not move.

If anyone attempted to alter the record — change a clock-in time, swap a GPS coordinate, modify the worker ID — the fingerprint check would fail immediately and the change would be visible to anyone who ran a verification. This is not a policy. It is the way the system works.

### Who can see my GPS location?

Your GPS is captured only at the exact moment you tap CLOCK_IN and at the exact moment you tap CLOCK_OUT. Two GPS points per shift. FLOSTRUCTION does not track your location while you are working, while you are on a break, or at any other time.

Your supervisor can see the GPS points for your shifts when they review them. FLOSMOSIS staff can see your GPS data only if you ask us to look at a specific shift as part of a dispute or support request. Nobody else has access.

The GPS data is used to confirm you were at the right site at the times you tapped. It is evidence for you as much as for anyone else — if someone questions whether you were on site, your GPS record answers that.

### What happens to my records if I leave my employer?

Your records stay with you. Sign into FLOSTRUCTION with the same phone number and your full shift history is still there. Your labour-hire company or employer cannot take your records when your employment ends.

Every company that uses FLOSTRUCTION signs a contract that requires your records to be kept for seven years from your last day of employment with that company. After that period, you will be notified before any deletion occurs. FLOSMOSIS holds your records separately from your employer's systems — they exist independently.

To download everything before you leave, tap "My records" then "Download" and choose CSV (opens in Excel) or PDF receipts (one page per shift). The CSV includes the fingerprint for each shift so the records can be verified anywhere, by anyone, at any time.

### I cannot log in — what do I do?

FLOSTRUCTION signs you in with your phone number and a one-time code sent by SMS. If the code is not arriving:

- Check that you have mobile signal — the SMS cannot reach you without it.
- Check that the phone number shown on the screen matches your current number.
- Check your SMS inbox for a message from FLOSMOSIS or a short-code number.
- Try tapping "Resend code" after 60 seconds if the first message has not arrived.

If you have changed your phone number since you last logged in, email support@flosmosis.com with your old number and your full name. We will verify your identity and update your account.

If the FLOSTRUCTION app is not loading at all, try opening it in your phone's web browser at the web address your employer gave you. FLOSTRUCTION runs in the browser — there is nothing to install from an app store, and a fresh browser window sometimes resolves loading issues.

### Where can I learn about my rights?

The [Your rights](/field/rights) page explains your rights as a FLOSTRUCTION worker in plain language. These include your right to see your own records at any time, your right to dispute a shift, your right to export your data, and your right to keep your records after leaving your employer. Each right includes a plain statement, how to use it in the app, and the legal basis.

For broader employment rights — pay rates, award entitlements, leave, and dismissal — the Fair Work Ombudsman at fairwork.gov.au is the free, official source. The Fair Work Infoline is 13 13 94. If you are a union member, your union delegate is another good first contact.
