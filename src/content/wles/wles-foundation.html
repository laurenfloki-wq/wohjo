The WLES Foundation

The WLES Foundation publishes and maintains the Workforce Labour Event Standard — an open, royalty-free technical standard for the cryptographic verification of labour events in contingent workforce arrangements.

Current status
The Foundation is in its formation phase. It operates as a governance framework administered by FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD (ACN 697 323 925), acting as sole Founding Member. The Founding Member has committed to causing the incorporation of the Foundation as a separate Australian legal entity within twenty-four months of the Charter's adoption.

Purpose

The Foundation exists to publish, maintain, and evolve the Workforce Labour Event Standard (WLES); to protect its openness and interoperability across implementations; to hold and make available the trade marks, specifications, test vectors, and governance documents necessary for the integrity of the standard; to support the development of a community of implementers, adopters, researchers, and advocates for verifiable labour record-keeping; and to represent WLES in engagements with standards bodies, government agencies, industry associations, and regulatory authorities.

The Foundation is not a commercial entity. It does not sell products, does not charge for implementation of WLES, and does not generate revenue from the adoption of the standard.

The standard

The current published version of WLES is v1.0. The specification, together with its authoritative test vectors and a reference verifier, is available at wles.io.

Any party may implement WLES without permission from or commercial relationship with the Foundation or its members. Implementations that satisfy all MUST-level requirements of the specification, and reproduce the authoritative test vectors, are conforming implementations.
