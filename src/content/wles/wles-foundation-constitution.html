Preamble

The Workforce Labour Event Standard (WLES) is published and maintained by the WLES Foundation as an open, royalty-free technical standard for the cryptographic verification of labour events in contingent workforce arrangements.

The Foundation exists to ensure that WLES remains open, interoperable, credibly neutral, and available to any party without commercial restriction, for the benefit of workers, employers, payroll and compliance systems, regulators, and the public.

This Constitution is the governing document of the Foundation during its formation phase. It establishes the Foundation’s purpose, membership structure, decision-making processes, and commitments regarding the evolution of the standard. It also records the Founding Member’s binding commitment to incorporate the Foundation as a separate Australian legal entity within twenty-four months of this document’s adoption.

1. Formation Phase Status

1.1 Current Structure

At the date of this document’s adoption, the WLES Foundation operates as a governance framework administered by FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD (ACN to be assigned on incorporation, Australian Capital Territory), acting as sole Founding Member. The Foundation is not yet a separately incorporated legal entity. This is the formation phase.

During the formation phase, FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD exercises the Foundation’s functions, holds the Foundation’s intellectual property in trust for the Foundation’s eventual incorporation, and maintains the Foundation’s public presence at foundation.wles.io.

1.2 Commitment to Separate Incorporation

The Founding Member commits, with binding effect, to cause the incorporation of the WLES Foundation as a separate Australian legal entity within twenty-four months of this document’s adoption. The legal form of the separately incorporated Foundation will be a company limited by guarantee under the Corporations Act 2001 (Commonwealth), with the intention of registration as a not-for-profit organisation.

This commitment is irrevocable except by unanimous vote of all Founding Members at the time the variation is proposed, plus a twelve-month public comment period.

1.3 Continuity Through Incorporation

On separate incorporation, all intellectual property, trade marks, domain registrations, specification publications, test vectors, governance records, and Foundation correspondence are to be transferred to the separately incorporated entity. The Founding Member undertakes to effect such transfer promptly upon incorporation and without consideration, on terms preserving the open-licence commitments of this Constitution.

1.4 The Constitution as Interim Governing Document

This document serves as the Foundation’s interim governing document during the formation phase. On separate incorporation, this Constitution is replaced by the formal constitution of the separately incorporated company limited by guarantee, which must incorporate all binding commitments in this document including those set out in clauses 5 and 8.

2. Purpose

The Foundation exists to:

  1. publish, maintain, and evolve the Workforce Labour Event Standard;

  2. protect the openness and interoperability of WLES across all implementations;

  3. hold and make available trade marks, specifications, test vectors, and governance documents necessary for the integrity of the standard;

  4. support the development of a community of implementers, adopters, researchers, and advocates for verifiable labour record-keeping;

  5. represent WLES in engagements with standards bodies, government agencies, industry associations, and regulatory authorities.

The Foundation is not a commercial entity. It does not sell products, does not charge for implementation of WLES, and does not generate revenue from the adoption of the standard. Operating costs during the formation phase are borne by the Founding Member. Post-incorporation funding arrangements are addressed in clause 6.

3. Scope of Authority

The Foundation has authority over the following:

  1. the text and publication of the WLES specification;

  2. the test vectors and conformance criteria defining a conforming implementation;

  3. the extension registry;

  4. the use of the WLES name and mark in relation to conformance claims;

  5. the governance process for amendments and new versions; and

  6. the transition of the Foundation’s role in the event of a successor body.

The Foundation does not have authority over:

  1. commercial implementations of the standard;

  2. pricing, product design, or business practices of implementers;

  3. disputes between commercial parties using WLES records; or

  4. jurisdictional regulatory matters concerning labour law, evidence, or data protection.

Implementers operate independently under whatever commercial terms they determine, subject only to the open-licence obligations of the standard itself.

4. Membership

4.1 Classes of Membership

The Foundation recognises four classes of Members:

Founding Members are the originating signatories of this Constitution and subsequent Members admitted to the Founding Member class under clause 4.2. Founding Members hold permanent seats on the Standards Council and participate in all amendment and versioning decisions requiring their consent.

Implementer Members are organisations that have published or deployed a conforming implementation of WLES. Implementer Members are entitled to participate in technical working groups, contribute to test vectors, and propose extensions.

Industry Members are industry associations, peak bodies, employer organisations, worker-side organisations (including but not limited to unions and worker advocacy groups), and other sector-representative bodies. Industry Members are entitled to participate in consultation processes and contribute formal position papers on proposed amendments.

Public Interest Members are academic institutions, research bodies, government agencies (in their policy-engagement capacity), and other organisations with a demonstrated public-interest stake in workforce verification. Public Interest Members are entitled to participate in consultation processes and advise on regulatory, research, and evidentiary considerations.

4.2 Founding Membership — Formation Phase

The founding Member of the Foundation at the date of adoption of this Constitution is FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD.

The Founding Member commits that sole Founding Member status will not remain indefinitely. Within three years of the publication of WLES v1.0 (that is, by 18 April 2029), the Foundation will admit to the Founding Member class:

  1. at least one independent Implementer Member, being an organisation that has deployed a conforming implementation of WLES and operates independently of FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD;

  2. at least one Industry Member, being an industry association or peak body relevant to the contingent workforce sector; and

  3. at least one Public Interest Member, being an academic institution or research body with demonstrated engagement in workforce verification research.

Admission to the Founding Member class requires the consent of existing Founding Members and is intended to occur as the natural evolution of the Foundation’s governance over time.

4.3 Admission of Non-Founding Members

Non-founding Members are admitted on application to the Foundation. During the formation phase, the admission process is administrative — applications are reviewed by the existing Standards Council and accepted unless there is substantive cause for refusal. Cause for refusal is limited to:

  1. applicant organisations that have failed to produce a conforming implementation claimed as the basis of membership;

  2. applicant organisations whose stated purposes are substantially incompatible with the open and interoperable nature of the standard; or

  3. applicant organisations with unresolved legal disputes with the Foundation or its Members concerning the standard.

4.4 Membership Obligations

Members of all classes undertake to:

  1. acknowledge the Foundation as the publishing authority of WLES in their public communications about the standard;

  2. comply with the trade mark usage guidelines published by the Foundation;

  3. participate in good faith in the governance processes defined in this Constitution; and

  4. disclose material conflicts of interest when participating in technical working groups or decision forums.

Members are not required to contribute financially or to make patents, copyrights, or implementations available to other Members beyond the commitments made in this Constitution.

5. The Standards Council

5.1 Composition

The Standards Council is the principal governance body of the Foundation. Its composition is:

  1. one seat per Founding Member;

  2. up to three elected seats held by Implementer Members, selected by vote of the Implementer Member class;

  3. up to two elected seats held by Industry Members, selected by vote of the Industry Member class; and

  4. up to two elected seats held by Public Interest Members, selected by vote of the Public Interest Member class.

During the formation phase, the Standards Council consists of the Founding Member alone, with elected seats remaining vacant until the respective Member classes are sufficiently populated to conduct elections.

5.2 Powers

The Standards Council is authorised to:

  1. approve new versions of the WLES specification;

  2. approve amendments to existing versions;

  3. approve changes to test vectors and conformance criteria;

  4. admit and remove Members;

  5. approve changes to this Constitution, subject to clause 9;

  6. appoint working groups for technical, legal, and operational matters; and

  7. make such other decisions as are necessary for the operation of the Foundation.

5.3 Decision Making

Routine decisions — admission of Members, approval of test vector updates, approval of non-breaking clarifications to specifications — are decided by simple majority of voting Council seats, provided at least one Founding Member votes in favour.

Substantive decisions — approval of new major versions of the specification, approval of breaking amendments, changes to this Constitution, transition of the Foundation to a successor body — require the affirmative vote of all Founding Members plus a simple majority of elected seats then filled.

The unanimous consent requirement for Founding Members is intended to protect the integrity of the standard against capture by any single commercial or regulatory interest during the early membership period. As the Founding Member class expands under clause 4.2, the unanimous requirement becomes a multi-party veto rather than a single-party veto.

5.4 Transparency

All decisions of the Standards Council are recorded in publicly accessible minutes, published at foundation.wles.io/council. Exceptions are made only for matters concerning individual legal, commercial, or personnel issues. Such exceptions are themselves recorded as having occurred even where the substance is not disclosed.

6. The Specification and Its Evolution

6.1 Publication

The current specification of WLES is published at wles.io. Prior versions remain accessible at version-specific URLs. The Foundation commits to maintaining accessibility of every published version for no less than fifteen years from its publication date.

6.2 Amendment Process

Proposals for amendment may be submitted by any party — Members and non-Members alike — via standards@wles.io. The Foundation categorises proposals as:

  1. Errata — corrections of non-substantive errors (typographical, referential, illustrative). Addressed by administrative publication; no Council approval required.

  2. Clarifications — non-breaking additions to specification text that clarify intended behaviour without changing any requirement. Approved by simple majority of the Council.

  3. Non-breaking additions — new fields, new event types via the extension mechanism, new SHOULD- or MAY-level requirements. Approved by simple majority, with a thirty-day public comment period.

  4. Breaking changes — alterations that invalidate conforming implementations of the current version. Require a new major version.

6.3 Version Lifecycle

A major version of WLES, once published, is supported by the Foundation for a minimum of five years. A successor major version, if introduced, does not supersede the prior version earlier than twenty-four months after the successor’s publication. During this parallel support window, both versions are fully supported and conforming implementations of either remain conforming. After the parallel window closes, the prior version enters legacy status, remaining available but no longer recommended for new implementations.

6.4 Commitment to Openness

The Foundation makes the following irrevocable commitments concerning WLES:

  1. every version of the specification, past, present, and future, is and will remain available under an open licence permitting any party to implement WLES without payment of royalties or licence fees;

  2. the Foundation will not assert patent claims against conforming implementations. Any patent rights held by the Foundation or its Members that read on a conforming implementation are licensed on a royalty-free basis;

  3. the Foundation will not introduce field-of-use restrictions on implementations. WLES may be used in any jurisdiction, for any lawful purpose, by any party;

  4. the Foundation will not tie conformance to commercial products or services. A conforming implementation is determined solely by compliance with the published specification and test vectors.

These commitments are binding on the Foundation and on any successor body to which the Foundation transfers its role. Amendment of these commitments requires unanimous Founding Member approval and a twelve-month public comment period.

7. Operations and Funding

7.1 Administrative Support During Formation Phase

During the formation phase, administrative support for the Foundation is provided by FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD at its own cost. This includes:

  1. maintenance of the foundation.wles.io and wles.io web presence;

  2. administrative processing of Member applications;

  3. coordination of amendment proposals and Council decisions;

  4. publication of specifications, test vectors, and Foundation communications.

Administrative support does not confer additional governance rights on the providing party beyond those held by Members of the relevant class.

7.2 Post-Incorporation Funding

On separate incorporation of the Foundation as a company limited by guarantee, the Foundation may accept voluntary financial contributions from Members to cover operating costs. Contributions are voluntary in the sense that they are not a condition of membership. Contributing Members receive no governance advantage over non-contributing Members.

7.3 Conflict of Interest

Where a Council member or working group participant has a material commercial interest in a decision under consideration, they must disclose the interest and may be required to recuse themselves from deliberation or voting. Conflict of interest procedures are detailed in a separate operating document published at foundation.wles.io/conflict-of-interest.

8. Trade Marks

The names “WLES,” “Workforce Labour Event Standard,” the WLES Foundation name and mark, and associated logos are held by the Foundation (or, during the formation phase, by FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD in trust for the Foundation). They may be used by Members and non-Members under the trade mark usage guidelines published at foundation.wles.io/trademarks.

Use of these marks to describe a conforming implementation is permitted without additional licence. Use that suggests endorsement by the Foundation of a particular commercial product, or that asserts conformance where conformance has not been demonstrated, is prohibited and may be enforced through ordinary trade mark mechanisms.

9. Dissolution and Succession

9.1 Dissolution

The Foundation may be dissolved only by unanimous vote of the Standards Council with all Founding Member seats filled and voting. In the event of dissolution, all Foundation assets — including trade marks, domain registrations, specification publications, test vectors, and governance records — transfer to a successor body nominated by the dissolving Council or, failing nomination, to an independent open-standards organisation selected by a neutral arbiter.

9.2 Succession During Formation Phase

If the administrative support provider (FLOSMOSIS PTY LTD during the formation phase) ceases operation or voluntarily withdraws from its administrative role before separate incorporation of the Foundation is completed, an alternative administrative arrangement must be established within six months. During any gap in administrative support, the Foundation’s existing publications remain accessible at their current URLs for no less than twelve months from the gap commencement.

9.3 Non-Abandonment

The Foundation cannot be effectively abandoned while any conforming implementation remains in active use. If governance becomes impaired and no Council is functioning, any Member or implementer may petition the Foundation’s successor body, or failing that, a court of competent jurisdiction, to appoint an interim administrator for the sole purpose of maintaining specification accessibility and trade mark integrity.

10. Amendment of This Constitution

This Constitution may be amended by unanimous approval of all Founding Members plus a two-thirds majority of elected Council seats then filled, following a sixty-day public comment period. Proposed amendments are published at foundation.wles.io/constitution-amendments during the comment period.

The commitments in clause 1.2 (separate incorporation), clause 6.4 (commitment to openness), and this clause 10 are subject to the additional amendment constraint described in clause 6.4: twelve-month public comment period in addition to unanimous Founding Member approval.

11. Effective Date and Signatories
